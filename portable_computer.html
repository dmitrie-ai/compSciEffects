The Portable Computer

A computer designed to be easily moved around and includes a display and keyboard.

IBM 5100 - first commercially sold portable computer

Brief History

The first commercially sold portable was the 50-pound (23 kg) IBM 5100, introduced 1975. The next major portables were Osborne's 24-pound (11 kg) CP/M-based Osborne 1 (1981) and Compaq's 28-pound (13 kg) 100% IBM PC compatible Compaq Portable (1983).

Laptops

Epson HX-20, the first "laptop"
The first "laptop" was the Epson HX-20. Invented in 1980 and released in 1982. It had an LCD screen, an 8-bit CPU, a rechargeable battery, and a small printer, in a 1.6 kg chassis. Laptop specific CPU's were designed to use minimal power, such as the 1990 Intel i386SL(32-bit).


Smartphones

IBM Simon- the forerunner of smartphones
The development of the smartphone was enabled by several key technological advances. One of them is the exponential decrease in size of MOSFETs during the 1990s–2000s. Other important enabling factors include the lithium-ion battery, invented in the 1980s and commercialized in 1991 and the development of more mature software platforms for mobile devices.

The first commercial smartphone was sold in 1994 under the name Simon Personal Communicator. In addition to placing and receiving cellular calls, it had a touchscreen, could send and receive faxes and emails. It included an address book, calendar, appointment scheduler, calculator, world time clock, and notepad, as well as other visionary mobile applications such as maps, stock reports and news. It featured an LCD and PC Card support.
HP OmniGo 700LX

In 1996, HP released the OmniGo 700LX. It had a 640×200 resolution CGA compatible four-shade gray-scale LCD screen and could be used to place and receive calls, and to create and receive text messages, emails and faxes. It was also 100% DOS 5.0 compatible, allowing it to run thousands of existing software titles, including early versions of Windows.
Nokia Navigator 9210

LG Prada- first large capacitive touchscreen phone
In 2001 Nokia released the Nokia 9210 Communicator. It was the first smartphone to run Symbian OS. It used a 32-bit 66 MHz ARM9-based RISC CPU, 8MB of RAM, 16MB of Internal storage with the ability to expand that by up to 16MB. It also allowed users to download third party apps.
In 2006 LG released the LG Prada which was the first phone with a large capacitive touchscreen. It had a 3" 240x400 screen
